USA

The United States of America is a union of 50 states in North America. It is one of the world's largest economies and is considered a mixed economy. That means it operates as a free market economy in consumer goods and business services. But, even in those areas, the government imposes regulations to protect the good of all. It operates as a command economy in defense, some retirement benefits, medical care, and other areas. The U.S. Constitution created and protects America's mixed economy.

There are a few key components of the U.S. economy. These different economic indicators help us understand how the U.S. economy is doing.

  • Gross domestic product (GDP): $22.7 trillion
  • GDP growth rate: 6.6%
  • Real GDP per capita: $58,454
  • Gross national income: $21.7 trillion PPP dollars
  • Unemployment rate: 5.4%
  • Minimum wage: $7.25 per hour
  • Currency: United States Dollar
  • Core inflation rate: 4.3%
